BSRG

A place for people to learn about computer systems together.

Computer systems are the building blocks of applications and the fabric that ties them together. (Databases, networks, virtualization, programming languages, compilers, distributed algorithms, optimizers, orchestrators, verifiers, libraries, …)

We meet weekly in Boston. Each week, we pick a different topic in computer systems, read about it, and discuss together.

If you're not an expert, you can still come by! We welcome people from all backgrounds. Technology is used by everyone, and diverse experiences enrich communal discourse. The goal here is to learn, not to make commercial software.

Computers are pretty cool; let's explore!

Weekly Topics

Each week we dive into a different topic. We read during the session because sharing knowledge with others is more fun, provides helpful context, and builds a community deeply valuing curiosity and individual empowerment.

Week 1 — Jan 12, 2025
Writing git from scratch

Git is a version control system used by most of the world's software developers. What's under the hood? Let's explore content-addressed storage and hash trees.

Commits are snapshots, not diffs• Write yourself a Git
Week 2 — Jan 19, 2025
Perspectives on async

Cooperative and preemptive multitasking, schedulers, concurrency vs parallelism. Case studies in Go and Rust internals.

What Color is Your Function?• How Goroutines Work• Async/Await
Week 3 — Jan 26, 2025
The database storage layer

Storage is at the heart of databases, lying below execution and query planning. Let's learn about them and build our own.

Disk-Oriented DBMS Overview• LSM Trees• Database Internals
Week 4 — Feb 2, 2025
Virtualization with KVM

Computers tend to produce more copies of themselves. Let's see how virtualization and emulation work on the OS and machine levels.

JSLinux• Virtio• Book of crosvm
Week 5 — Feb 9, 2025
Structured data encoding

Schema-based binary formats for specifying and serializing structured data. Design tradeoffs for RPCs, data archiving, and OLAP.

Protocol Buffers Encoding• Cap'n Proto• Apache Arrow
Week 6 — Feb 16, 2025
Compression with zstd

Exploring state-of-the-art lossless data compression. How do you pack big things in a small package, fast?

LZ77 Exploration• Zstd blog post• Zstd spec
Week 7 — Feb 23, 2025
Linux executables

What goes into executables and dynamic linking? How much is engineered versus operating system magic?

Making our own executable packer
Week 8 — Mar 2, 2025
Memory allocators

A discussion of general-purpose memory allocators. We'll focus on jemalloc and the newer mimalloc.

jemalloc (2006)• mimalloc (2019)
Week 9 — Mar 9, 2025
io_uring

Reading about an up-and-coming Linux subsystem for high-performance async I/O. Memory access models and buffer ownership.

Lord of the io_uring• Notes on io-uring• tokio-rs/io-uring
Week 10 — Mar 16, 2025
Distributed training

Training big ML models is the elephant in the room. Everyone starts by talking about compute, but it ends up being mostly about networking.

Allreduce (2017)• How to Train Really Large Models on Many GPUs?
Week 11 — Mar 23, 2025
FoundationDB

A distributed, transactional key-value store that underpins several new database systems. Strict serializable and lock-free.

FoundationDB (SIGMOD '21)• Technical Overview
Week 12 — Mar 30, 2025
Memory models

A series on how memory consistency is preserved by multicore processors: in hardware, programming languages, and Go.

Memory Models (rsc)
